Bolgnese Recipe

Description

Bolognese is a classic Italian meat sauce, traditionally made with ground beef,
pork, and vegetables, often including onions, carrots, and celery. It's slow-cooked to develop rich flavors,
and may also include white wine, milk, tomato paste, and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 dry white wine
  • half a stick of butter
  • 1 lb 80/20 fat ground beef
  • 28-ounce can whole peeled tomatoes (san marzano)
  • 1 white onion
  • 2-3 carrots
  • 2-3 celery stalks
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • fresh rosemary, thyme, bayleaf
  • garlic powder, onion powder, dried basil, dried oregano
  • nutmeg, salt, pepper
  • pappardelle pasta

Steps

  1. Finely dice up carrot, onion, celery (not mush) and garlic.
  2. In a large pot over medium heat, melt butter and cook garlic until fragrant (1-2 mins)
  3. Throw in your chopped onion and cook for a few minutes
  4. Now add your carrot and celery until the veggies become soft. Season with salt and pepper
  5. Add in your ground beef, breaking it up and seasoning with more salt and pepper
    also feel free to add in garlic powder and onion powder
  6. Once the ground beef is cooked, add in 2 cups of whole milk. Let this reduce until no liquid remains.
  7. Add in 2 cups of dry white wine and again let his reduce until no liquid remains.
  8. Once reduced, add in your canned whole tomatoes, crushing them with your fingers.
  9. In the tomato can, pour in chicken stock or water and pour into pot
  10. Add in your sprig of thyme, rosemary, and bayleaf
  11. Let this reduce over low heat for 3-4 hours. If the sauce looks like it will burn, feel free to add water or chicken stock to keep it going.
  12. Once reduced, boil your noodles according to the package.
  13. Serve with noodles, heating up with a pad of butter over the stove top if you wish.
